## Context & positioning

Paragon Machine Works is a subcontractor specialized in small parts for frame builders: head tubes, derailleur hangers, cable routing. The workshop is divided into a turning zone and a milling zone. Very high machine density for the available floor space. Founded by Calvin's father, now managed by Calvin Norstad.

**Production flow**

> **Raw material (titanium / aluminum)** → **Cutoff** → **Multi-axis CNC turning** → **Multi-vise CNC milling** → **Deburring (vibratory machines)** → **Inspection** → **Anodizing<br>(subcontractor)** → **Finished goods inventory** → **Shipping on order**

## Workshop organization

Two distinct zones: turning and milling. Between the two, a conventional lathe and mill for internal tooling and personal projects. **Multi-vise per machine** for batch machining (setup time reduction). Part drawings attached to each machine with **magnetic clips**. **3D validation (Prusa printing)** before any production run. Vibratory machines for systematic deburring.

## Production management analysis

Mixed production: order-based + finished goods inventory (many standardized references for frame builders). Titanium raw material purchased opportunistically in bulk. Pragmatic decision: **no finishing on non-functional surfaces** = significant time savings. **Strong specialization in a single segment** = captive and loyal clientele.

**Strengths**

- "Parts for frame builders" niche = unique expertise and captive clientele
- Multi-vise per machine: setup time reduction
- 3D validation before production = zero rework on production runs
- No finishing on non-functional surfaces

**Areas for improvement**

- Russian/Chinese titanium dependency = geopolitical supply chain risk
- Underdeveloped visual PO management

## Key takeaway

Specialization in a highly targeted customer segment (frame builders) allows developing very sharp expertise and strong loyalty. The niche protects from mass competition and justifies solid margins.
